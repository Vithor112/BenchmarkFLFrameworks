Very cautious of the bias that AI models can have 

The bias is introduced by the lack of diversity in the training data. 

A large portion of AI to radiology is being developed in the context of single institution and it can be hard to generalize these AIs.

Pickup LC, Gleeson F, Talwar A, Kadir T. Lung nodule risk stratiﬁ-
cation using CNNs: can we generalize from screening training data?
Presented at: Conference on Machine Intelligence in Medical Imaging;
September 26-27, 2017; Baltimore, Maryland

This study above shows that a AI for detecting cancer in pulmonary nodules in CT had a higher perfomance with a US dataset than a Oxford one.

The patient population being quite different from institution to institution is a factor that can potencialize this bias.

The law to protect the privacy of patients prompt many institutions to not share data, making a diverse training dataset more difficult.

It also show that is important to validate and monitor the algorithm perfomance.
